The Bugis Museum dedicated in sharing the stories of Malays especially Bugis ethnic particularly in Malaysia's Peninsular. Explore the history and culture of the Bugis in Malaysia through the Bugis Museum!

Know The Owner

Abdullah Ahmad, the owner of the Bugis Museum, creating the museum with the intention to preserve the cultures and histories of Bugis community by telling and showing the visitors all about them. He hopes that it can be learned and remembered by modern society.

The museum was originally founded in 1982 as Bugis Heritage. On July 26, 1994, the Johor Government supported the museum through the Johor Heritage Foundation and declared it the first Bugis Museum in Malaysia. In October 2016, the Governor of South Sulawesi, Syahrul Yasin Limpo visited this museum and met the local Bugis. He hoped that relations between the Bugis in different countries would be maintained.

A Brief of
Bugis Museum

There are over 2,000 artifacts on display at this museum! Including weapons, clothes, old ceilings, jewelry, pictures, paintings and many more!


This museum is housed in a building in the form of traditional Malaysian architecture.


The museum is open daily from 10:00 a.m. until 6:00 p.m. Admission is free.
